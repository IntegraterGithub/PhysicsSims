Physics - Collisions

The basics of collisions are as follows:

  • Inelastic collisions

    • An inelastic collision is defined as being a collision where the two objects stick together. This is because the some of kinetic energy of the particles is dissipated to the surrounding. However, even though the kinetic energy is not conserved, the momentum is by the conservation of momentum. This states the total momentum before and after any collision must be the same. The formula for momentum is:
    p = m v 2

    Meaning:

    p i = p f
    m t ( v i 1 + v i 2 ) = m t ( v f 1 + v f 2 )

  • Elastic collisions

    • Elastic collisions are similar to inelastic collisions, in the sense momentum is conserved, but in elastic collisions the kinetic energy is conserved. Now, given that kinetic energy is:

    K = 1 2 m v 2
    K i = K f
    1 2 m t ( v i 1 + v i 2 ) = 1 2 m t ( v f 1 + v f 2 )

    Now doing using simultanoes equations we could use this to solve elastic collision problems.
